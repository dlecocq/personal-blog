I was reading about Algorithm Geeks</a>, a Google Group dedicated to algorithm-related questions apparently. I looked at one of the topics, Unique Elements in an Array</a>, wondering what the chatter looked like.

To remove duplicate elements of an array, the best way I know is to sort your array, and then do a linear traversal, comparing each to its successor, and when they match, deleting the one of them. Quicksort is generally the algorithm-of-choice in large part because of its simplicity, but someone on this thread suggested heapsort</a> - an algorithm of which I had never heard. I looked it over, and was drawn to the article on heaps</a>.
